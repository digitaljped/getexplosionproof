# About getExplosionProof by Super Cool

## One of the Last Independent Specialists Standing

The explosion-proof air conditioning market has consolidated dramatically over the past decade. Most of the companies that once competed in this space have been acquired, folded into larger conglomerates, or simply stopped doing the work. What's left is a handful of operators — and we're one of the few that remains independent.

That independence matters to you as a buyer. We don't answer to a parent company's quarterly targets. We don't push you toward a specific product line because someone upstream told us to. When we recommend a Bard wall-mount over a mini split, or a pressurization system over a full explosion-proof buildout, it's because that's what your application actually calls for.

getExplosionProof is operated by Super Cool Company — a specialty industrial HVAC distributor focused on products and applications that most HVAC companies won't touch. Explosion-proof air conditioning is the most technical, most specialized corner of that market, and it's where we've built our deepest expertise.

## What We Do

We connect buyers who need explosion-proof cooling with the fabrication expertise to do it right. When you contact us, you're not talking to a general contractor who occasionally handles hazardous location work. You're talking to specialists who have worked through hundreds of Class 1 and Class 2 applications across refineries, chemical plants, mines, pharmaceutical facilities, and military installations.

We handle the entire process from first call to delivery:

- Helping you confirm or interpret your hazardous location classification
- Specifying the right unit type and size for your application
- Coordinating fabrication with our specialist manufacturing partner
- Providing the documentation package you need for compliance, procurement, and audit

Whether you need a single 1-ton Bard wall-mount for a remote wellhead enclosure or a fully redundant 20-ton pressurized system for a refinery control building, the process starts the same way — a conversation.
